Color rules · non-negotiable

  • Default surface is navy-900 for hero/anchor sections, cream-50 for body sections.
  • Default text is cream-50 on navy and navy-900 on cream. Match the contrast pair to the surface.
  • Gold is the primary accent — money figures, italic wordmark emphasis, decorative rules, eyebrow underlines, premium badges, key CTA on dark surfaces.
  • Two accent instances maximum per fold. Gold may repeat 2–3×; claret 0–1×.
  • Pure white is forbidden as a section fill. Pure black is forbidden as a text color.
  • No bright cyan, no neon, no electric blue. Brand reads institutional, not tech-forward.
  • Gradients are forbidden as section fills. The single permitted gradient is a 2% noise-textured navy in hero blocks.
  • Claret is reserved for genuine urgency — statute deadlines, time-sensitive offers. Maximum one instance per fold. Never as a button fill.

Italic is sacred

Italic is reserved for Instrument Serif headlines and pull quotes. It is the brand's emotional voice — use it on one to three words per headline, never more.

Never italicize body copy. Never italicize Inter. The italic-gold "More" in the wordmark is the single most important design decision in this system.

All-caps is reserved

All-caps is reserved for JetBrains Mono. Never set Inter or Instrument Serif in all-caps. Tracking on mono UPPERCASE eyebrows is generous (0.08–0.1em). That is what makes it read institutional rather than tech.

Numbers are data

Stat callout numerals at display sizes use Instrument Serif for the premium register. JetBrains Mono only at body-stat sizes. "$30,000+", "10 seconds", "55–65%" all read as data, not prose.

Constraints

Body lines stay under 70 characters — constrain max-width on text columns. Headlines never exceed 12 words. If you need more, you have two headlines.

Construction

Clear space & minimum size

Minimum clear space equals the cap-height of the first letter on all sides. The wordmark's three-word lockup is fixed — Sign in navy, More in italic gold, Cases in navy.

Minimum legible size for the wordmark is 120px wide on screen, 1.5in in print. Below that, switch to the square mark.

The mark's gold underline is 6px stroke at 200×200 and scales proportionally — never below 1.5px stroke.

04 / Components
Buttons · stats · quotes · cards · forms

Sharp 4px corners. No shadows. One CTA per surface.

The brand reads architectural — flat, decisive, premium. Border radius 4px on buttons, inputs, cards. Never above 12px. Borders 1px on cream-200 or navy-700, 1px gold-500 for emphasis. Shadows only when absolutely required.

Buttons

Three button styles, never mixed on the same surface. Primary on cream is ink-fill; primary on navy is gold-bordered ghost. Approved CTA copy is reserved — never "Learn more," "Get started," or "Free PI Review."

05 / Voice & tone
Engineer-not-marketer · premium institutional

Sound like the team that built the thing — not the team that sells it.

Senior engineer at a frontier lab writing a launch post. Anthropic explaining a technical capability. A private bank's annual report. Confident, plain-spoken, technical when it earns its keep, premium without being precious.

Seven principles

01

Direct

Lead with the claim. Proof follows. Never bury the lede.

02

Technical, not jargony

Sophisticated buyers respect specificity. They distrust vagueness.

03

Engineer-not-marketer

Sound like the team that built the thing, not the team that sells it.

04

Short sentences

Single ideas per line. Cut the connective tissue.

05

Specific numbers

"Sub-10-second response," "14 minutes from click to signed retainer." Not "fast" or "industry-leading."

06

Confident, not boastful

State what is true. Let the reader infer the implication.

07

Buyer-respectful

Managing partners are sophisticated buyers. Do not explain category basics. Do not flatter.

09 / Compliance
Bar exposure · FTC §255 · TCPA

Never guarantee outcomes. Never call leads "clients."

PI marketing has bar-association, FTC, and state-licensing exposure. These rules are non-negotiable. Every public surface carries a footer disclaimer. Any client deployment requires a state-specific compliance pass before publish.

Rule 01 / Outcome guarantees

Never: "We will sign 20 cases for you."

Always: "We deliver pay-per-signed-retainer infrastructure."

Rule 02 / Vertical terminology

Use leads, prospects, matters, retainers.

Never call leads "clients" until the engagement has converted.

Rule 03 / Professional advice

Never imply legal advice from the platform. We are marketing and intake infrastructure, not a law firm. Footer must make this clear.

Rule 04 / FTC §255 testimonials

Every testimonial must be either real and consented or visibly labeled "Design-matched illustration of representative client outcomes."

Rule 05 / Required footer disclaimer · paste verbatim

Sign More Cases provides marketing and intake infrastructure for personal injury law firms. We are not a law firm and do not provide legal advice. Performance figures cited reflect the experience of representative client firms. Individual results vary based on geography, case type, ad spend, and intake operations.

Always include the "results vary" disclaimer on any volume or revenue claim. Footer of every page.

FAQ
What managing partners ask

Six questions. Plain answers.

What does pay-per-signed-retainer actually mean?
You do not pay for leads, names, intakes, or hand-offs. You pay when a retainer signs. Period. The infrastructure carries the risk because it earns the right to. We measure cost-per-signed against your historical CPL on the strategy call so the math is clear before you commit.
How is this different from Quintessa or other lead-gen agencies?
Most agencies are reselling Vapi or GoHighLevel with a logo on top. We built the call infrastructure in-house. The team is Stanford, Harvard, and MIT engineers from frontier labs. Speed is not a feature on our deck — it is the thing we ship. Sub-10-second first contact, average 14 minutes from click to signed retainer.
Which sub-verticals do you cover?
Auto, premises, mass tort, med mal, workers' comp. The AI qualifies for case type and statute fit at first contact and routes only retainable matters to your intake team. Single-track competitors send everything; we send what your firm actually files.
What does onboarding look like?
Two weeks from signed agreement to first routed retainer. Week one is intake-flow mapping and AI training on your case-type criteria. Week two is parallel running against your existing intake to calibrate qualification thresholds. By day 14 we are sole-routing on whichever ad accounts you elect.
What if a lead does not sign?
You do not pay. There is no monthly retainer, no per-lead fee, no setup charge. The only invoice that hits your books is when a retainer signs and the engagement is in your CRM. If volume drops below the floor we agreed to on the strategy call, we owe you the gap.
Are you a law firm?
No. Sign More Cases provides marketing and intake infrastructure for personal injury law firms. We are not a law firm and do not provide legal advice.
