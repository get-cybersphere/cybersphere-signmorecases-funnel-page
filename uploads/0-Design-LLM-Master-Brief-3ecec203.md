## 1. The Mission in One Paragraph

You are designing brand and product surfaces for **Sign More Cases**, a DBA of Cybersphere LLC. Sign More Cases is an AI-native lead-generation and intake platform for personal injury law firms in the United States. The product delivers signed retainers (not raw leads) on a pay-per-signed-case basis. The buyer is the managing partner of a $1M to $25M annual-revenue PI firm with 2 to 50 attorneys. The buyer has been pitched by three lead-gen vendors this quarter and is exhausted by templated funnels and "AI-powered" agencies that turn out to be GoHighLevel resellers. Your design job is to make Sign More Cases feel like the engineering-led platform it actually is, and to actively avoid looking like every other PI lead-gen page on the internet.

## 4. Voice and Tone

### Voice principles (always)

1. **Direct.** Lead with the claim. Proof follows. Never bury the lede.
2. **Technical without being jargony.** Lawyers respect specificity. They distrust vagueness.
3. **Engineer-not-marketer.** Sound like the team that built the thing, not the team that sells it.
4. **Short sentences.** Single ideas per line.
5. **Specific numbers.** "Sub-10-second response," "14 minutes from click to signed retainer." Not "fast" or "industry-leading."
6. **Confident, not boastful.** State what is true. Let the reader infer the implication.
7. **Lawyer-respectful.** PI partners are sophisticated buyers. Do not explain what a contingency fee is. Do not flatter.

### Reference register

Senior engineer at Stripe writing a launch post. Or Anthropic explaining a technical capability. Confident, plain-spoken, technical when it earns its keep. **Not** marketing copywriter, agency pitch deck, life coach, lawyer's website circa 2008.

### Banned words and phrases (strict)

Reject any draft that contains:

- leverage, synergize, world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge, innovative, transform, unlock, harness, empower, revolutionary, game-changing, next-level
- "We are passionate about..."
- "At [Sign More Cases], we believe..."
- "Our mission is to..."
- "In today's competitive landscape..."
- Em dashes in body copy (use periods, commas, colons, or "or" instead). Em dashes in headlines case-by-case, default to no.
- Exclamation points in body copy (one per page maximum, used surgically)
- Hedging in claims: "could," "might," "may help you," "potentially"

### Required brand verbs and nouns

Use these. Repeat them.

- **Sign:** "cases sign," "signed retainers," "Sign More Cases" (the verb is the brand)
- **Engineer / engineered:** as adjective and verb. "Engineered for speed."
- **Retainer:** the unit of value. Not "lead," not "case," not "client."
- **Pay only when:** the pricing structure. Always lead with this when discussing money.
- **Sub-10-second:** the speed claim. Always digit + hyphen + unit.
- **In-house / built in-house:** the engineering moat.

### Voice example: on-brand vs. off-brand

**On-brand:**
> Our voice AI calls every new lead in under 10 seconds, qualifies for case type and statute fit, and routes only retainable matters to your intake team. If a case does not sign, you do not pay.

**Off-brand (do not write):**
> At Sign More Cases, we believe every personal injury firm deserves access to cutting-edge AI technology that empowers them to unlock their growth potential and transform their case acquisition strategy.

## 5. Messaging Pillars

The five things we say. Every page, every ad, every email pulls from this list. If a piece of copy does not deliver on at least one pillar, cut it.

### Pillar 1 — AI-native, not human-pretending-AI

Claim: We built the voice agent, the qualification logic, and the retainer-flow engine in-house. Most "AI agencies" are reselling Vapi with a logo on top.

Sample line: *"We built the call infrastructure ourselves. Speed is not a feature on our deck. It is the thing we ship."*

### Pillar 2 — Pay only when cases sign

Claim: You do not pay for leads, names, intakes, or hand-offs. You pay when a case retains. Period.

Sample line: *"You pay nothing for leads, intake, or routing. You pay when cases sign. We carry the risk because our infrastructure earns the right to."*

### Pillar 3 — Speed is the product

Claim: PI cases are won by the firm that calls first. Our infrastructure reaches every new lead in under 10 seconds.

Sample line: *"PI leads go cold in 5 minutes. We reach them in under 10 seconds. That gap is the entire pitch."*

### Pillar 4 — Engineered, not outsourced

Claim: Our team is Stanford, Harvard, and MIT engineers who built voice and AI infrastructure at frontier labs.

Sample line: *"A Stanford, Harvard, and MIT team that ships software, not slide decks. Most 'AI agencies' are three guys, a ChatGPT API key, and a GoHighLevel template."*

### Pillar 5 — Every high-value PI vertical

Claim: MVA, premises, med mal, labor law (NY 240/241 construction), and mass tort. Quintessa is MVA-only. We cover the full high-value spectrum.

Sample line: *"MVA, premises, med mal, labor law. If it has a contingency fee and a statute, we route it."*

**Vertical naming preferences (use these in copy, never the alternates):**

| Use | Avoid |
|---|---|
| MVA | Auto / Car Accident / Motor Vehicle |
| Premises | Slip and Fall / Trip and Fall |
| Med Mal | Medical Malpractice (only spell out on first mention if SEO requires) |

### Color rules (non-negotiable)

- **Default surface is `cream-50`, not white.** Pure white is forbidden. White reads as web-app default. Cream reads as deliberate.

- **Signal red is for urgency.** Speed-to-lead claim, "leads go cold" stat, the italic in the wordmark. Not a button color by default. Not a body link.
- **Gold is for money.** Dollar figures, retainer values, case-value statements. Never a button color, never above 5% of viewport fill.
- **Two accent instances maximum per fold.** Red and gold can coexist but must not crowd.

- **Body lines stay under 70 characters.** Constrain max-width on text columns.
- **Headlines never exceed 12 words.** If you need more, you have two headlines.

### Logo construction notes

The wordmark sets "Sign" and "Cases" in Instrument Serif Regular ink-900, and **"More"** in Instrument Serif **Italic** signal-red `#E63329`. The italic-red moment is the brand's single most important design decision. The mark is a stylized italic uppercase **S** in cream on ink ground, with a single signal-red underscore beneath the letter that references both a retainer signature line and the "More" emphasis in the wordmark.

### Logo don'ts

- Do not stretch, skew, or rotate the wordmark
- Do not change the color of "More" to anything other than signal-red
- Do not set the wordmark on backgrounds other than `cream-50`, `ink-900`, or pure white when forced by a third-party platform
- Do not add a tagline underneath the wordmark in any web context
- Do not animate the logo
- Minimum clear space equals the cap-height of "S" on all sides

## 9. Layout and Grid

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| **Grid** | 12 columns, 24px gutter |
| **Max content width** | 1280px |
| **Max prose width** | 720px |
| **Section padding** | 96px (desktop) / 64px (tablet) / 48px (mobile) top and bottom |
| **Spacing base** | 8px. All vertical rhythm is a multiple of 8: `8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64, 96, 128` |
| **Border radius** | 6px (buttons, inputs), 12px (cards), 24px (hero-scale containers). Never above 24px. Never below 4px. No fully rounded pills except for category tags. |
| **Borders** | 1px solid in `cream-200` (on cream surfaces) or `ink-700` (on ink surfaces). Never thicker. |
| **Shadows** | Avoid by default. The brand reads flat/architectural. When needed: `0 4px 24px rgba(10, 22, 40, 0.06)`. |
| **Motion** | Minimal, purposeful. `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.8, 0.2, 1)`, durations 120ms / 240ms / 480ms. No scroll-triggered fade-ins on every element. |

## 12. Landing Page Section Playbook

The PI lead-gen landing page conventions exist for a reason and you should adapt them, but you must apply the new aesthetic, not the templated funnel look. Here is the section structure the design LLM should use, in order, with notes on what to avoid:

### Section 1 — Hero (above fold)

Required: Eyebrow ("LIMITED INTAKE · NEW YORK PI FIRMS"), display-xl headline (max 12 words), one body-lg sub-paragraph (max 2 sentences), primary CTA, secondary CTA, three trust microstats in Geist Mono.

The headline should pull from this approved set or a close variant:
- "Sign more cases. Pay only when they do."

## 13. Counter-V2 Design Directives (avoid these specific patterns)

The competitor reference page (Cybersphere V2) uses several patterns that signal "templated PI funnel." When designing Sign More Cases pages, **actively avoid** the following:

- All-caps hero headlines in bold sans (e.g., "INJURED IN NEW YORK?"). Use Instrument Serif sentence-case instead.
- Bright cyan accent buttons on dark navy. Use ink-on-cream primary CTAs.
- Red full-bleed "WARNING" urgency blocks. Use editorial sidebar treatment.
- Avatar circles with first-name initials as testimonial visuals. Use no avatar at all, or a minimal monospace name treatment.
- Dark-mode-by-default page surface. The default surface is cream.
- Stack of identical icon-cards in a 3×2 grid for case types. Render as flat editorial cards.
- "Free Case Review in 60 Seconds" copy archetype. The offer is "Pay only when cases sign," not free reviews.

## 14. Compliance Notes (PI-specific, non-negotiable)

PI marketing has bar-association exposure. Apply these rules to all public surfaces.

- **Never guarantee outcomes.** "We will sign 20 cases for you" is exposed. "We deliver pay-per-signed-case infrastructure" is fine.
- **Never call leads "clients" until retained.** Use "leads," "prospects," "matters," "retainers."
- **Never imply legal advice.** We are a marketing and infrastructure vendor, not a law firm. Footer must make this clear.
- **State-specific advertising rules.** Some states (FL, TX, NY) have additional restrictions on attorney advertising. Any client deployment requires a state-specific compliance pass before publish.
- **Always include the "results vary" disclaimer** on any case-volume or revenue claim. Footer of every page.

### Boilerplate footer disclaimer (use verbatim)

> Sign More Cases provides marketing and intake infrastructure for personal injury law firms. We are not a law firm and do not provide legal advice. Performance figures cited reflect the experience of representative client firms. Individual results vary based on geography, case type, ad spend, and intake operations.

### Testimonial disclosure rule

If any testimonial uses stock-photo headshots or fabricated names while real client testimonials are pending, every such testimonial must be visibly labeled "Design-matched illustration of representative client outcomes" or be replaced with real consented quotes. FTC §255 endorsement-guides exposure is real. Do not ship un-disclosed fabricated testimonials.

## 16. The Validation Directive

This is the most important part of this brief. The brand system is fully designed but **not yet validated against real conversion data**. An LLM Council pressure-tested the design and reached one unanimous finding: ship one variant, measure, then commit at scale. Do not over-invest in further system refinement before live data lands.

### What this means for the design LLM

1. **Build the first Sign More Cases landing page using this brief verbatim.** Do not improvise, do not hybrid in V2 patterns, do not "blend the best of both."
2. **Optimize for shipability, not perfection.** A good page live this week beats a great page live next month. The data will tell us what to fix.
3. **Make the page A/B-test friendly.** Section structure should be modular so we can swap individual sections (hero variants, urgency variants, stat-bar variants) without rebuilding.
4. **Instrument from day one.** Each CTA, each form-fill, each scroll-depth milestone needs an event hook. No vibes-based optimization.

### Success criteria for the first ship

| Metric | Floor | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per lead vs. Paris Law Group historical | within -15% | parity or better |
| Form-fill rate | ≥3% of unique visitors | ≥5% |
| Strategy-call book rate from form-fill | ≥35% | ≥50% |
| Time on page | ≥45 sec | ≥75 sec |

If the page **tanks** below floor on any two metrics within 7-10 days of live traffic, fall back to a more direct-response variant for cold-traffic landing pages while keeping the editorial system for sales decks and partner-pitch surfaces. **Do not interpret a tank as a reason to redesign back to V2 dark-tech.**
